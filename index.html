GMO Crops

GMO crops are crops that have been genetically modified. It involves inserting DNA into the genome of a crop. This can give it new characteristics and can even make it resistant to diseases!

Advantages to GMO Crops

  • Higher yields
  • Longer shelf life
  • Resistant to diseases and pests
  • Has better taste
  • Grows faster
  • Healthier to eat
  • Fewer pesticides, land, and water is used
  • Cheaper to buy
  • More nutritious

Disadvantages to GMO Crops

  • May cause unexpected allergic reactions
  • May increase antibiotic resistance
  • May have an increased production of toxins

GMO Crops have their advantages and disadvantages, but it is most definitely more efficient than selective breeding and cross-breeding.
